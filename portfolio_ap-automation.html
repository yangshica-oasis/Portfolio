The Problem

Powerful tool, hard to use

Sage Intacct had strong AP capabilities — but the experience was fragmented, manual-heavy, and difficult to navigate. Customers were processing invoices at $20 per bill on average, taking 14 days per invoice, with 31% of vendor payments arriving late.

The goal: an overall refresh of the AP digital experience — building a streamlined bill-to-payment cycle through reduction of manual steps and task-driven activities.

Research Findings

What was breaking down for users

🔀
No Single Solution
All businesses had unique invoice-handling processes. Users preferred a single solution within Intacct for invoice storage, voting, and approvals — but had to stitch together multiple tools.
🔍
Limited Filter Capability
Filter capability was quite limited, making it difficult for users to surface the invoices and bills most relevant to their role or current task.
🔌
Disconnected Information
Information was disconnected — users had to navigate to different places to view payments, posted payments, and payment requests, with no unified view.
📍
No Status Visibility
No clear indication of the status or progress of a bill. Users couldn't tell at a glance where a bill was in the workflow or what action was needed next.
🖱️
Too Many Clicks
Required too many clicks to complete even a small task — just to find the details of a bill took excessive navigation through menus and sub-pages.
📋
No Organization of Transactions
No grouping or organization of transactions. Bills, payments, and approvals were listed without structure, making prioritization and triage nearly impossible.
The People

Four roles, one connected process

AP doesn't belong to one team. It spans clerks processing bills, accountants managing approvals, controllers monitoring cash position, and vendors tracking payment status. The redesign needed to serve each role's distinct mental model while keeping them in a shared, connected workflow.

Redesign Goals

Three principles driving the redesign

The redesign wasn't just about surface-level improvements. Three strategic principles shaped every design decision — from the task-driven dashboard architecture to the bill details experience.

Goal 01
Holistic Experience
Evaluate the experience as a whole. Build an end-to-end workflow that aligns overall business goals with end-user needs — no more disconnected views, no more context-switching between unrelated screens.
Goal 02
Intelligent & Scalable
Help customers reduce operational cost through automation and smart coding. Build a system that's scalable as customer needs change — not a rigid set of screens, but a flexible framework for diverse AP workflows.
Goal 03
Engaging Process
Solve real customer problems: inefficient processes, late payments, high operational cost, and error-prone data entry. Replace friction with clarity — clear status, clear next actions, and a workflow that feels like progress.
Design Highlights

Key decisions that changed the experience

Dashboard · Task Orientation
From information dump to task-driven home base
The original AP dashboard showed raw data with no guidance on what to do next. The redesigned dashboard surfaces tasks directly — "10 draft bills pending review," "6 approved payments ready to deliver" — giving every role an immediate, actionable view of their workload. The monthly summary and vendor cash flow chart provide the financial context controllers need without requiring a separate report run.
Bill Details · Source Comparison
Side-by-side validation of the original invoice
One of the most common errors in AP was data-entry mistakes when transcribing paper invoices into the system. The redesigned bill details view attaches the original invoice alongside the digital record — letting clerks validate vendor name, amounts, and terms against the source document without switching windows. Attachment visibility controls let users show or hide the original as needed.
Bill List · Before → After
A list that actually helps you work
The original bill list was a flat table with fixed columns and no way to prioritize or filter by what mattered. The redesigned list introduced configurable columns, column-level filters, direct links to bill details, and secondary line-level actions — so AP clerks could triage their queue without navigating away from the list. Status pills and sorting made it immediately clear what needed attention first.
Success Metrics

How we defined success

The usability study was designed to validate the redesign against a specific set of user behavioral goals — measuring the reduction in friction, task time, and error rates across the AP workflow.

Task Speed
% increase in users finding and launching tasks within 5 minutes
Dashboard surfaced tasks directly — reducing time to action from navigation to a single click.
Payment Speed
% increase in users creating draft payments in 5 minutes
Streamlined bill-to-payment flow with clear status steps and direct navigation from task list.
Manual Entry
% decrease in time spent entering bills manually
Side-by-side original invoice view and smart coding reduced transcription errors and rework.
Data Quality
% decrease in users fixing duplicates or wrong data
Validation UX caught mismatches at entry time, reducing downstream correction cycles.
Information Access
% increase in users finding bill and payment details within 5 minutes
Connected bill detail view unified payments, approvals, and attachments in one place.
Late Payments
% decrease in users paying late vendor bills
Task-driven dashboard with priority indicators surfaced at-risk bills before deadlines were missed.
