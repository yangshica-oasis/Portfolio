Project Overview

From Excel chaos to a rules-driven pricing platform

Roku's Retail Strategy and Sales Ops teams manage TV and player promotional pricing across a 13-week rolling roadmap for every major retailer — Best Buy, Amazon, Target, and others. The entire process lived in Excel, with price changes cascading manually across dozens of SKUs, retailer-specific exports reformatted by hand, and late-breaking changes buried in Slack threads with no audit trail. This project set out to replace that with a unified, rules-driven calendar platform.

Initial Design

From insight to interface

Two use cases drove the initial design: (1) converting the master pricing roadmap into customer-specific retailer roadmaps based on agreed terms, and (2) the weekly change workflow — a 4-step collaboration across Retail Strategy, Sales Ops, and FP&A. Four core screens were prioritized to unblock the highest-pain workflows first.

AI Prompt — Initial Design Direction

"I'm designing a retail pricing calendar platform for Roku's Sales Ops team. The two primary use cases are: (1) uploading a master pricing roadmap and auto-deriving customer-specific roadmaps based on per-retailer terms (MSRP, invoice MSRP, margin %, trailing credits), and (2) a weekly change workflow: Retail Strategy initiates a MAP change → Sales Ops forecasts impact (1-2 days) → FP&A approves → change communicated to retailer. Suggest the 4 highest-priority screens, what each should show, and which UI patterns (editable table, approval stepper, formula preview, weekly grid) fit each best."

↗ View design flow

Pricing Roadmap View

Weekly grid showing MSRP and MAP (promo price) per SKU. Past weeks are locked (view-only); rolling weeks are editable. Price changes auto-cascade across the product family with manual override capability. Filters by segment, screen size, and program name.

↗ View design flow

Customer Terms Manager

Per-retailer configuration for MSRP, invoice MSRP, margin %, trailing credit rules, and accrual percentages. Best Buy and Amazon require unique calculation structures; all other retailers share a standard pattern. Static once agreed — updated only at negotiation.

↗ View design flow

13-Week Retailer Roadmap

Auto-generated customer-specific roadmap derived from the master pricing roadmap + agreed terms. Supports split-week events natively (Prime Day, Black Friday). One-click export to customer-ready PDF or Excel — eliminating manual reformatting for each retailer.

↗ View design flow

Change Workflow & Audit Log

4-step collaborative workflow: Retail Strategy initiates MAP change → Sales Ops forecasts unit impact (1–2 days) → FP&A approves cost implications → change communicated to retailer. Every locked-price change is tied to a timestamped audit record — no more Slack threads as the paper trail.
